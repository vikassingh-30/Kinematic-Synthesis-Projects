HOW IT WORKS
4-Bar Linkage — Step by Step
Read each step below and follow along. Even a kid can do it! 🚀
🎯
STEP 1
Drop 5 Target Dots on the Canvas
  • Think of 5 places a robot arm should touch — those are your targets
  • Click anywhere on the white drawing area to drop a colored dot
  • Drop them one by one — they will be named C1, C2, C3, C4, C5 automatically
  • Made a mistake? Click and drag any dot to move it to a new position
  • Keep dots spread apart — they must not sit on top of each other!
  • Once all 5 dots are placed, press Proceed to Step 2 → in the sidebar
🖱️ Move the view: Right-click + drag OR hold Shift + drag to pan left/right/up/down  |  Scroll wheel to zoom in or out
🔵
STEP 2
Pick 2 Pairs of Dots to Find the Rocking Hinge (Hr)
  • Click any 2 of your 5 dots — a blue line will appear exactly in the middle between them
  • Now click 2 more different dots — an orange line appears in the middle of those two
  • Where the blue and orange lines cross is the special point Hr
  • Hr is the fixed hinge on the ground where the rocking arm is attached
  • Once both pairs are picked and Hr appears, press Proceed to Step 3 →
  • Picked the wrong dot? Press Undo Last Selection to go back one step
🖱️ Move the view: Right-click + drag OR hold Shift + drag to pan  |  Scroll wheel to zoom
🌀
STEP 3
Spin the 3 Glowing Lines Until They Line Up
  • You will now see 3 glowing lines joined like a fan, spinning around Hr
  • Left-click + drag the drawing area left or right to spin the fan
  • Use the angle slider in the sidebar for tiny fine-tune movements
  • Your goal: spin until the yellow line in the middle (T₂) exactly covers the orange dashed line (B₂)
  • When they match, the badge turns purple — or press Snap to Align to jump there in one click
  • Once lined up, press Proceed to Step 4 →
🖱️ Move the view: Right-click + drag OR hold Shift + drag to pan  |  Scroll wheel to zoom
⚙️
STEP 4
Grow Circles to Find the Spinning Hinge (Hc) and End Point B
  • Drag the R slider on the left to make circles grow or shrink around your dots
  • When the circle from the first dot from Pair 1 touches a glowing tracing line, a small crossing dot appears — that is the first cut point
  • The same circle size from the first dot from Pair 2 gives you the second cut point
  • A line drawn through the middle of those two cut points will hit the tracing line at Hc — the spinning hinge!
  • Press Draw Hc Circle — you will see a large circle that the spinning arm follows
  • Press Congruent △ — this draws helper triangles to find point B (the tip of the rocking arm)
  • Use Near Cut / Far Cut buttons to choose which crossing dot to use when two appear on the line
  • When Hc and B are both shown on screen, press Finish Synthesis →
🖱️ Move the view: Right-click + drag OR hold Shift + drag to pan  |  Scroll wheel to zoom
🎬
STEP 5
Watch Your Robot Arm Draw the Path!
  • Press Form Linkage — the 4 arms of your machine will snap together!
  • The green arm spins around Hc (this is called the crank)
  • The blue arm rocks left and right around Hr (this is the rocker)
  • The orange arm connects the crank and rocker (this is the coupler)
  • The orange dot on the orange arm is the pen tip — it should pass through all 5 of your target dots! 🎉
  • Press Animate to start the motion. Drag the speed slider to go faster or slower
  • Toggle Primary / Crossed to try two different ways to connect the arms
🖱️ Move the view: Right-click + drag OR hold Shift + drag to pan  |  Scroll wheel to zoom
